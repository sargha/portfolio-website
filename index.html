Inspired by many breadboard videocard builds online and especially Ben Eater’s on YouTube I went on a journey to try and recreate the VGA build he has done. But to me that felt way too boring, recreating something that had already been done. I wanted to take it an extra step further. Instead of building a low resolution video card that outputs at less than 640p, I set of on a journey to build a video card that would output using HDMI at 1920x1080p instead. This turned out to be harder than I thought it would be. Changes to Generic Video Card Project      1. HDMI output instead of VGA      2. Higher output resolution of 1920 x 1080 (1080p)      3. Ability to playback full videos and not only display a static pitcture Troubles/Blocks and Questions with Project       1. HDMI uses a digital signal instead of an analog signal, how would I deal with that on TTL chips?      2. 1080p has a higher pixel frequency of 148.5Mhz compared to that of 640p at 40.00Mhz      3. How would I deal with that many bits of data?       4. I started with 4 Bit counters, how will I scale this up?  With all of these questions to answer I set out to build my Breadboard Video Card. Get ready this is going to be fun.

Michigan Tickets App
iOS App for students to buy and sell tickets safely
The first version design was simply a test to find the balance of correct colors to use for the app, as well as to design the simplest user interface for this app. However as time went on with my design things got a bit more complicated. I began to draw inspiration from the design of the Venmo app and thus resulted in a version two design much like that. Working with Adobe XD, I was able to create an iPhone mockup for version 2 of my design.
This iOS App like many of my projects is still a work in progress. I have the mindset that even the best designs and the best projects can always be made better when looked at from a different perspective.  At the University of Michigan, especially during our football season, many students rely on a Facebook  group to buy and sell their student tickets.  Problems this App Solves:       1. Getting scammed by sellers who are not University of Michigan Students      2. Not getting paid the correct amount by a buyer      3. Not getting the ticket, thus losing money      4. Having to text multiple people in order to find a ticket in your price range      5. Not knowing what a good price to sell your ticket at  How the App Solves Issues:      1. Verifies that seller is a University of Michigan student via UM Email      2. Money is not sent to seller, until both seller and buyer confirm transfer of ticket      3. One place to see all available tickets available for sale       4. Estimated pricing option for sellers based on the time the ticket is posted
